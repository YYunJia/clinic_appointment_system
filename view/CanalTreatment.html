What is Root Canal Treatment?

Root canal treatment (also called endodontics) is needed when the blood or nerve supply of the tooth (known as the pulp) is infected through decay or injury.

During the procedure, the infected or inflamed pulp is removed and the inside of the tooth is carefully cleaned and disinfected, then filled and sealed with a rubber-like material called gutta-percha. Afterwards, the tooth is restored with a crown or filling for protection.

Contrary to popular belief, root canal treatment is no more painful than having a filling placed. Our modern techniques and anesthesia ensure you're comfortable throughout the procedure.

The Check-Up Process

1

Examination & X-Ray

We examine your tooth and take X-rays to assess the damage and plan the treatment.

2

Anesthesia

Local anesthesia is administered to ensure you feel no pain during the procedure.

3

Pulp Removal

The infected pulp is carefully removed from the tooth's chambers and canals.

4

Filling & Sealing

The cleaned space is filled with biocompatible material and sealed to prevent reinfection.

Benefits of Root Canal Treatment

  • Preserves Your Natural Tooth - Maintains your natural smile and prevents adjacent teeth from shifting
  • Efficient Chewing - Restores normal biting force and sensation, allowing you to eat comfortably
  • Natural Appearance - Crowns are customized to match the color and shape of your natural teeth
  • Cost-Effective - More affordable than tooth extraction followed by a bridge or implant
